Call for Tutorials

Overview

ADBIS 2025 invites submissions of tutorial proposals. The topic of a tutorial should be related to the scope of the ADBIS conference. Tutorials on interdisciplinary topics, novel topics, and significant applications, as well as tutorials with hands-on, are also encouraged.

A tutorial proposal should cover state-of-the-art research, development, and (if possible) tools. In particular, the proposal should include:

  • the title of the tutorial
  • names, affiliations, and email addresses of the presenters
  • the content description of the tutorial
  • target audience and its assumed background
  • related tutorials and the added value provided by the proposed tutorial w.r.t. the related ones
  • short CVs of presenters, with a note on their background in the area of the tutorial.

Tutorials will be selected based on their technical quality, significance of the topics, relevance to ADBIS.

Accepted tutorials will be included in the ADBIS 2025 Springer LNCS proceedings.

Important Dates

  • Submission deadline: April 18, 2025
  • Notification of acceptance: May 12, 2025
  • Camera-ready tutorial description: June 13, 2025

Submission

A tutorial proposal must be submitted in the pdf format, to each of the tutorial chairs, by email:

  • Georgia Koloniari gkoloniari@uom.edu.gr
  • Robert Wrembel robert.wrembel@put.poznan.pl

Proposals must be formatted according to the Springer LNCS authorsâ€™ instructions, available at: https://www.springer.com/gp/computer-science/lncs/conference-proceedings-guidelines, preferably in LaTeX, of maximum length of 8 pages, including ALL material.

A proposal violating the length, file type, or formatting requirements will be desk rejected. It is suggested that a submission adhered to the Diversity and Inclusion Guidelines adopted by the ADBIS community.
